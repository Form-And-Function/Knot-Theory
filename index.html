Braid

  • A set of n strings, all of which are attached to a horizontal bar at either end bottom
  • The string always heads in one direction from the top to the bottom bar (ie cannot make a U turn and travel back up)

Ambient Isotopy

Two braids are “equivalent” if we can rearrange them to look the same without passing the strings through others or themselves, keeping the top and bottom bars fixed, and the strings attached to those bars. Think of these as strings in real life that you can “untangle” or stretch to change their 2D projections.

Braid Closures

We can pull the bottom bar around and glue it to the top bar to form a knot or link. This is the closure of a braid.

Alexander’s Theorem

All knots and links can be represented as closed braids.
  • Proved in 1923
  • Procedure: Find a point in the plane about which the strings of the link always turn in the same direction “Open up” braid.
  • Yamada-Vogel alternate algorithm of transforming knot/links into closed braid.
  • How do we know when the closures of two braids represent the same link?
